Olafur Eliasson – “Ice Watch”

Art and ecology: bringing melting ice into the city to make people see climate change.

Who is he?

Olafur Eliasson is a Danish-Icelandic contemporary artist. He mixes art, science and nature to make people feel environmental issues.

The artwork: “Ice Watch”

He placed 12 real blocks of ice from a Greenland fjord in public spaces and let them melt. People could touch and listen to the ice.

Where and when?

Copenhagen (2014), Paris during COP21 (2015), London (2018). Shown in the street so everyone can see it.

The message

The melting ice is a direct symbol of global warming. Instead of numbers, people watch climate change happen in front of them.

Type of art

Environmental installation art: contemporary, temporary, in public space, made to provoke emotion. The art disappears – like the ice.

Impact

The work created media attention and public discussion. Eliasson shows art can support science and ecology.

Conclusion

“Ice Watch” makes climate change visible, touchable and emotional. Art here is not decoration – it is a warning.
