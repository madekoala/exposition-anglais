Art & Ecology
Source: Olafur Eliasson / olafureliasson.net

Introduction

We present a contemporary artwork that denounces the ecological crisis by using real ice in the city.

Goal: show how art can create an emotional reaction to climate change.

The artist
Source: local file (portrait of Olafur Eliasson)

Who is he?

Olafur Eliasson is a Danish-Icelandic contemporary artist who mixes art, science and nature.

He often works in public space and focuses on environmental topics.

The artwork
Source: Hyperallergic – “Ice Watch, Paris 2015”

“Ice Watch”

12 real blocks of ice from a Greenland fjord, placed in public areas and left to melt.

People could touch, listen and watch the ice, turning climate change into a direct experience.

Context
Source: The New Yorker – “Bringing Icebergs to Paris”

Where and when?

Copenhagen (2014), Paris during COP21 (2015), London (2018).

Shown in the street to reach a large audience, not just museum visitors.

The message
Source: Future / TechRadar image report on Ice Watch

Message

The melting ice is a direct symbol of global warming.

Instead of numbers, people watch climate change happen in front of them → more impact.

Type of art
Source: local file (Wikimedia – Ice Watch, Paris 2015)

Environmental installation

Contemporary, temporary, public, and designed to provoke emotion.

The piece disappears like the ice → the disappearance is part of the meaning.

Impact
Source: Yellowtrace – Olafur Eliasson Studio, Berlin

Impact

Media attention + public debate about climate change.

Art and science work together to make climate data understandable.

Conclusion
Source: Hyperallergic – “Ice Watch, Paris 2015”

Conclusion

“Ice Watch” makes climate change visible, touchable and emotional.

Art becomes a warning and a call to action.
