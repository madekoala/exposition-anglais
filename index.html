Art & Ecology

Olafur Eliasson – “Ice Watch”

Art and ecology: bringing melting ice into the city to make people see climate change.

This installation uses real ice from Greenland to create an emotional reaction to global warming.

The artist
Source: local file (portrait of Olafur Eliasson)

Who is he?

Olafur Eliasson is a Danish-Icelandic contemporary artist. He mixes art, science and nature to make people feel environmental issues.

His art is often public, immersive and focused on ecology.

The artwork
Source: Hyperallergic – “Ice Watch, Paris 2015”

The artwork: “Ice Watch”

He placed 12 real blocks of ice from a Greenland fjord in public spaces and let them melt.

People could touch, listen and actually watch climate change happening in front of them.

Context
Source: The New Yorker – “Bringing Icebergs to Paris”

Where and when?

Copenhagen (2014), Paris during COP21 (2015), London (2018).

Shown in the street so everyone can see it, not only people in museums.

The message
Source: Future / TechRadar image report on Ice Watch

The message

The melting ice is a direct symbol of global warming.

Instead of numbers, people watch climate change happen in front of them.

Type of art
Source: local file (Wikimedia Commons – Ice Watch, Paris 2015)

Environmental installation art

Contemporary, temporary, in public space, made to provoke emotion.

The art disappears – like the ice – to reinforce the ecological message.

Impact
Source: Yellowtrace – Olafur Eliasson Studio, Berlin

Impact

The work created media attention and public discussion.

Eliasson shows art can support science and ecology by making people care.

Conclusion
Source: Hyperallergic – “Ice Watch, Paris 2015”

Conclusion

“Ice Watch” makes climate change visible, touchable and emotional.

Art here is not decoration – it is a warning.
